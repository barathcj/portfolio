Tango is a prototype for a news website created for HackCMU. It presents news in different layouts based on the distance the viewer is from the screen. It uses the web camera to judge estimates the distance of the user's face from the screen. Based on the distance it has three different layouts (detail view, browse view and full screen video) Tango was awarded the 'Most Innovative Project' at HackCMU 2013.

We expect a beta version of the site to released by march 2014.

Tickolo is a prototype for a ticket exchange platform in which users can buy and sell spare tickets at less than face value. This system is ideal for people looking for last minute ticket deals. This app currently works on the android platform and a beta version will be launched by the end of January 2014.

akshay Based Mobile Device Management There is a need to control a user’s mobile phone inside an office, museum, research lab, theatre etc. Some companies would like their employees to not use the mobile camera inside office, or a cinema hall can have a policy to not let viewers record the movie. During my time at Samsung, we proposed an efficient and fast method by which a mobile device can be controlled inside a particular premise via near field communication (akshay). We also proposed that there is scope of development of a standard message exchange protocol for policy enforcement over akshay channel.

At Samsung, I was part of a team which developed a client side BAYESIAN SPAM FILTER It was designed to optimize storage space by utilizing time frame distribution and blacklisted domains to preset the attributes of Naïve Bayesian classification. This mechanism also aims at reducing the scope of tokenization of email, SMS messages based on the training data thereby reducing the power consumption related to processing of messages in the Android OS.
